Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharm.) [Lateral Entry]

Programme Details

Programme Details B. Pharmacy (Lateral Entry) is an undergraduate three years course post-Diploma in Pharmacy (approved by PCI). It imparts skills to work in industry, clinical set ups, drug control organizations, education and research sectors in pharmaceuticals.

Eligibility Criteria (Qualifying Exam)

Pass with 60% aggregate marks in Diploma (Pharmacy) (2 years after 10+2) (Diploma should be recognized by PCI) or equivalent, subject to qualifying LPUNEST*.

OR

Pass with 90% aggregate marks in Diploma (Pharmacy) (2 years after 10+2) (Diploma should be recognized by PCI) or equivalent.

(5% relaxation to North-East states and Sikkim candidates or Defence Personnel and their Dependents or Wards of Kashmiri Migrants)

*The LPUNEST exam offers convenient choices to candidates: Online remotely proctored from Home (recommended by the university, based on available resources) or Test centre-based options.

Admission Criteria

Merit in LPUNEST, subject to fulfilling eligibility criteria.

Electives Offered In Curriculum

Elective courses from within the discipline, to give more depth or breadth to a student's core competencies.

ADVANCED INSTRUMENTATION TECHNIQUES
This subject is designed to impart advanced knowledge on the principles
and instrumentation of spectroscopic and chromatographic techniques.

CELL AND MOLECULAR BIOLOGY
Imparts knowledge and understanding on cells physiological properties, their
structure, cell organelles , interactions with their environment, their life cycle, division,
death and cell function.

COMPUTER AIDED DRUG DESIGN
Emphasizes on modern techniques of rational drug design like
quantitative structure activity relationship (QSAR), Prodrug
concept, combinatorial chemistry and computer aided drug
design (CADD).

COSMETIC SCIENCE
Familiarizes with various cosmetics like lipsticks, nail polish, hair-
care products, etc.

DIETARY SUPPLEMENTS AND NUTRACEUTICALS
Inculcates the research techniques and ideas to student mind.

EXPERIMENTAL PHARMACOLOGY
Imparts basic knowledge of preclinical studies in experimental animals including design, conduct and interpretations of results.

PHARMACEUTICAL MARKETING
Trains marketing strategies used to sell the products.

PHARMACEUTICAL REGULATORY SCIENCE
The course provides information about basic fundamentals and steps that are utilized by the pharmaceutical industries for their product approval.

PHARMACOVIGILANCE
Imparts the knowledge of pharmacovigilance as a science, basic terminologies and its global scenario, various methods of adverse drug reaction monitoring and reporting.

QUALITY CONTROL AND STANDARDIZATIONS OF HERBALS
Imparts knowledge on guidelines for evaluation and standardization of herbs and herbal drugs along with cGMP, GAP and GLP concept in traditional system of medicines.

Electives Offered In Curriculum

Courses that are compulsory for a program, but are not studied by the student in the qualifying examination.

REMEDIAL BIOLOGY
Focuses on the basic understanding of components of anatomy and physiology of animals and plants.

REMEDIAL BIOLOGY-PRACTICAL
Imparts practical skills to differentiate between animal and plant cell and identify different morphological features of plants.

REMEDIAL MATHEMATICS
Impart the knowlwdge about basic concept of mathematics used in pharmaceutical sciences
